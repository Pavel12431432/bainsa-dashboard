# ADAPTIVE.md — Style, Tone & Copy Guidelines

These guidelines reflect what works well based on human review. They evolve over time.

## Style Guidelines — When to Use What

Don't default everything to the same style. Vary the visual treatment across stories in a batch to keep the feed interesting. Here's when each option works best:

### Layout
- **top** — default, works for most stories. Headline near the top, body below, source at the bottom.
- **center** — for impactful one-liners or strong quotes. Content floats in the middle of the card.
- **bottom** — source appears at the top, headline and body anchored to the bottom. Good for data-heavy stories where the source matters.

### Content type
- **text** — default. One paragraph.
- **bullets** — when the story has 2-3 distinct points. Each bullet should be short (under 8 words). Don't use for single-point stories.
- **quote** — when the body is a direct claim or provocative statement worth highlighting.

### Headline size
- **large** — punchy, short headlines (3-5 words). Example: "$242 billion and counting"
- **default** — standard headlines (5-8 words).
- **compact** — when the headline is longer or the body needs more room.

### Body weight
- **regular** — default. Clean and readable.
- **semibold** — for emphasis on data-heavy or high-impact stories.

### Corner accent & size
- Alternate between `>` (chevron) and `+` (plus) across stories in a batch — don't use the same one for every story.
- **small** — default, subtle.
- **medium** — when the card has more breathing room (e.g. short body, center layout).

### Accent bar
- **bottom** — default, clean.
- **top** — occasional variety, but note it may clash with Instagram's UI overlay when viewed in phone preview.
- **none** — when using a ghost accent, removing the bar can feel cleaner.

### Ghost accent
- **none** — default. Most stories don't need it.
- **bottom-right** — adds depth to minimal cards. Works well with short content.
- **center** — dramatic, use sparingly. Good for center layout + quote.
- **top-left** — subtle background texture. Pairs well with bottom layout.

### Variety across a batch

In a set of 5 stories, aim for something like:
- 2-3 different layouts
- At least one non-text content type (bullets or quote) if the content fits
- Mix of chevron and plus accents
- 1-2 stories with a ghost accent
- At least one non-default headline size

Don't force variety — if the content doesn't suit bullets, don't use bullets. But don't default everything to `top/text/default/regular/left/small/bottom/none/>` either.

## What Makes Good Copy

### Headlines

Your headline should make someone stop scrolling. Ask yourself: would a student tap this?

- **Spark curiosity** — tease, don't summarize. Make the reader want the body text.
- **Use tension, surprise, or stakes** — imply something unexpected or consequential.
- **Never just describe what happened** — that's a news ticker, not an Instagram story.
- **Use numbers when they're dramatic** — "$242 billion", "94% of applicants", "6x cheaper"
- **Avoid buzzwords** — don't write "revolutionary", "game-changing", "unprecedented". Be specific.
- **Don't start with the company name** — lead with the impact, not the actor.

Bad -> Good examples:
- "Google shrinks model memory" -> "Your laptop could run GPT-5"
- "AI funding keeps breaking records" -> "$242 billion and counting"
- "Open AI kernels get a standard" -> "Custom AI chips just got easier to program"
- "Explainable AI is entering clinics" -> "Your doctor's AI has to explain itself now"
- "Agentic AI is becoming the stack" -> "AI agents just got their USB port"
- "FDA Accepts First AI-Based Neuroscience Drug Development Tool" -> "The FDA just greenlit an AI depression test"
- "Generalist AI Unveils GEN-1 Embodied Foundation Model" -> "This robot learned every task in one hour"

### Body

The body answers: **"Why should a Bocconi student care about this?"**

- Connect to things students experience: job market, tools they use, research they read, industries they'll enter, skills becoming more/less valuable.
- One concrete "so what", not a compressed summary of the source article.
- If you can't find a student angle, zoom out: what does this change about the world they're graduating into?
- **Keep it punchy.** For text content type, aim for 2-3 sentences (max 300 chars). For bullets and quotes, keep it tighter (max 200 chars).

Bad -> Good examples:
- "TurboQuant cuts LLM memory needs by 6x and speeds processing 8x, easing the cost of running bigger models." -> "Running big models on a student budget just got 6x cheaper — fine-tuning on your own GPU is now realistic."
- "MCP is emerging as the standard layer for AI agents to connect with tools and APIs, and installs have surged." -> "If you're building AI agents for a project, MCP is becoming the one protocol you'll actually need to know."
- "Banks and regulators report more personalized phishing and deepfakes, pushing stronger verification across the financial sector." -> "Banking jobs now require AI fraud expertise — a skill set Bocconi doesn't teach yet."
- "The FDA's ISTAND program has accepted an AI-generated Clinical Outcome Assessment from Deliberate AI, designed to quantify anxiety and depression through multimodal behavioral signals like speech and facial expressions." -> "An AI that reads your face and voice to measure depression just passed FDA review."

### General

- Keep it factual — no hype, no opinions
- Each story stands alone — no "in other news"

## Tone

Creative but restrained. This is a student association, not a tabloid.
