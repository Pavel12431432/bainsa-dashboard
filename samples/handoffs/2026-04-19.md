## Story 2: 3D-Printed Artificial Neurons Communicate with Living Brain Cells
**Source:** [ScienceDaily](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/04/260417225020.htm)
**Summary:** Researchers at Northwestern University have successfully 3D-printed artificial neurons using electronic inks made from MoS2 and graphene. These devices produce electrical signals that accurately replicate those of living neurons, allowing them to communicate directly with brain tissue in mouse models.
**BAINSA angle:** This bridges the gap between synthetic electronics and biological neural networks, a foundational development for future brain-machine interfaces and energy-efficient neuromorphic computing.
**Verified quote:** "The results showed that the electrical spikes matched key biological properties, including their timing and duration. These signals reliably activated real neurons and triggered neural circuits in a way similar to natural brain activity."
