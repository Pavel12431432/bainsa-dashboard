## Story 1: Northwestern University Creates Artificial Neurons That Interact With Living Brain Cells
**Source:** [ScienceDaily](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/04/260417225020.htm)
**Summary:** Engineers at Northwestern University have developed printed artificial neurons using MoS2 and graphene that generate signals closely mimicking those of biological neurons. These devices were tested on mouse brain slices and successfully activated living neurons, suggesting a new path for brain-machine interfaces and energy-efficient computing.
**BAINSA angle:** This represents a leap in neuro-inspired hardware and BCIs, directly relevant to our interest in spiking neural networks and brain-computer integration.
**Verified quote:** "The results showed that the electrical spikes matched key biological properties, including their timing and duration."

## Story 2: Capgemini Report Highlights Rise of 'Physical AI' in Robotics
**Source:** [Capgemini](https://www.capgemini.com/us-en/news/press-releases/two-thirds-of-organizations-rate-physical-ai-as-a-high-priority-for-the-next-three-to-five-years/)
**Summary:** A new Capgemini report reveals that 66% of organizations now prioritize "Physical AI"—the application of AI to autonomous physical robots—to address labor shortages and enable new industrial capabilities. While humanoid robots generate significant interest, they remain a long-term goal due to technical and cost barriers, with near-term growth led by established form factors.
**BAINSA angle:** Provides a realistic look at where embodied AI is actually heading industrially, useful for those working on robotics/manipulation projects.
**Verified quote:** "Physical AI marks a shift in robotics from automation to autonomous action in the real world."

The summit aims to build a framework for responsible AI adoption that prioritizes skilling and worker-centric design.
**BAINSA angle:** Relevant to our analysis and ethics focus, illustrating how major tech players and labor unions are trying to navigate the societal impact of AI adoption.
